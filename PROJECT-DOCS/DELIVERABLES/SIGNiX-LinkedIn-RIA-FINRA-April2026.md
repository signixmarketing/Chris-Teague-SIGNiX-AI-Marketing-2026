## POST BODY

FINRA is examining how your firm supervises electronic signatures.

Three firms learned this the hard way. Over $3.6 million in fines across enforcement actions from 2022 to 2025. The same pattern in each case.

It was not a software failure. The platform worked fine. The problem was the firm had no system to detect red flags before the examiner did.

FINRA Rule 3110 requires more than an audit trail. It requires you to monitor that trail and act on what you find.

The red flags regulators look for: shared IP addresses across signers, signature links sent to the representative's own email, multiple clients signed within seconds of each other, authentication answers pulled from the client's own file.

These are not sophisticated hacks. They are supervision failures. And they are detectable before any exam, if you have the right controls in place.

SIGNiX Fraud Alert scans your e-signature audit trails for exactly these patterns. It notifies your compliance team before a regulator asks the question.

If your firm uses electronic signatures, your supervisory system needs to keep up.
