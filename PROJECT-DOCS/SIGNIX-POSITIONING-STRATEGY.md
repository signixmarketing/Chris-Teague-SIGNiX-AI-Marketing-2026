# SIGNiX Positioning Strategy: Multi-Sector Expansion

**Last updated:** April 2, 2026  
**Purpose:** Strategic framework for expanding SIGNiX beyond financial services into healthcare, mortgage, and insurance. Includes the core positioning argument, messaging philosophy, partnership-first angle, and the internal research needed to sharpen execution.

---

## The Three-Sector Story

SIGNiX wins where three conditions overlap:

1. Regulation requires a defensible audit trail — not just a signature
2. Identity verification matters — it's not enough to know someone clicked
3. The cost of a disputed or fraudulent signature is high

**The three sectors that meet all three conditions:**

> **Financial Services. Healthcare. Real Estate / Mortgage.**

This is not three separate campaigns. It is one argument applied to three audiences:

> *"A click is not a signature. Proof of identity is what makes it defensible."*

That statement works in a FINRA exam, a HIPAA audit, and a mortgage fraud dispute. It is the center of gravity for all positioning work going forward.

---

## Why "Security Hasn't Worked" — and What to Do Instead

### The real diagnosis

The management team's instinct is partially right. The problem is not that security is the wrong message. The problem is how security has been communicated.

| Version | How it sounds | Why it fails |
|---------|--------------|--------------|
| Old: feature-first | "SIGNiX uses PKI-based digital signatures." | Requires the buyer to care about PKI before they care about you |
| Better: fear-first | "Your client says they didn't sign that wire transfer. Can you prove they did?" | Leads with the consequence. Security becomes the answer, not the pitch. |

The fear-first version has not been fully tested. What failed was the feature-first version.

### The shift to make

Stop leading with what SIGNiX is. Start leading with what happens when you don't have it.

**The message to test:**
> "When your signature gets challenged in a FINRA exam or a patient dispute, SIGNiX gives you a CSM who already knows your workflow — and a document that proves who signed."

This does two things at once:
- Fear-first opens the door (legal defensibility, not security features)
- Partnership closes the deal (a human who knows your workflow, not a ticket queue)

## The Partnership-First Angle

### Why it works

SIGNiX has something DocuSign and Adobe Sign cannot credibly claim: **humans who stay involved after the sale.** CSMs learn a customer's workflows, stay engaged, and solve real problems. That is rare at SIGNiX's price point.

**The line to test:**
> "Most e-sign vendors sell you software and disappear. We assign you a person."

This line does three things:
1. Attacks the competition implicitly without naming them
2. Positions SIGNiX's size as an advantage, not a weakness
3. Differentiates on the dimension DocuSign literally cannot compete on

### Where it applies most

The partnership angle is especially powerful for the **TruStage FI depth lever** — existing customers who have SIGNiX via TruStage but haven't activated authentication. These buyers don't need a sales pitch. They need a CSM-led conversation: "Here's what you already have. Here's how to turn it on." That is relationship activation, not marketing.

### The two-part formula

| Part | Message | Role |
|------|---------|------|
| Fear-first | "One disputed signature costs more than years of SIGNiX." | Opens the door |
| Partnership-close | "Your CSM knows your workflows before a problem happens." | Closes the deal |

## Industry Expansion: Full Analysis

### Sector 1: Healthcare / HIPAA

**Why urgent now:**  
HIPAA requires covered entities to document patient consent, authorizations, and treatment decisions with verifiable identity. The HHS Office for Civil Rights is actively enforcing — fines run into the millions. Telehealth has normalized remote signing at scale, but most platforms use click-to-sign with no identity verification.

**Specific workflows:**
- Patient consent forms
- HIPAA authorizations
- Release of information requests
- Provider credentialing
- Telemedicine onboarding

**Fear trigger:**  
A patient claims they never authorized a procedure. "They clicked a link" is not a defense in an OCR audit.

**Key message:**  
> "HIPAA requires you to prove who consented. Not just that someone clicked."

**Urgency level:** High. Compliance officers in health systems are actively evaluating this.

---

### Sector 2: Real Estate / Mortgage

**Why urgent now:**  
Mortgage fraud is up significantly in 2025–2026. AI-generated fake IDs at closings are a documented, growing problem. The CFPB and state regulators are tightening requirements on identity verification at closing. Most DocuSign and Adobe Sign setups do not do real-time ID verification — SIGNiX's KBA and biometric auth do.

**Specific workflows:**
- Loan closings
- Title transfers
- HELOCs and refinance packages
- Wire authorization

**Fear trigger:**  
One fraudulent closing can cost a lender $200K–$500K in clawbacks and legal fees. RON is already in SIGNiX's DNA — this sector is adjacent to existing capability.

**Key message:**  
> "A closing is only as good as the identity behind it."

**Urgency level:** High. Overlaps with SIGNiX's existing RON product.

---

### Sector 3: Insurance

**Why urgent now:**  
State insurance commissioners regulate exactly how policyholder consent is obtained. Bad actors frequently forge beneficiary changes and policy cancellations to redirect payouts. One disputed life insurance payout without provable identity is a litigation and regulatory event.

**Specific workflows:**
- Beneficiary change forms
- Policy elections and cancellations
- Claims releases
- Broker of record letters
- Producer appointments

**Fear trigger:**  
A beneficiary dispute on a life insurance payout. If the insurer cannot prove the policyholder authenticated the change, they are exposed to litigation and state regulatory action.

**Urgency level:** Medium-high. Strong fear trigger, but sales cycles are longer than financial services.

---

### Sector 4: Legal / Law Firms (Partner play)

**Why it matters:**  
Courts are increasingly requiring stronger proof of identity for high-stakes documents. DocuSign's known weakness — proving who signed vs. proving that someone with email access clicked — is most visible in estate disputes, settlement challenges, and family law.

**Specific workflows:**
- Engagement letters
- Settlement agreements
- Estate planning documents (wills, trusts, powers of attorney)
- Litigation hold notices

**Best approach:** Flex API channel through a legal tech partner rather than direct sales. One integration reaches thousands of law firms.

---

### Sector 5: Human Resources / Employer Compliance (Partner play)

**Why it matters:**  
EEOC, DOL, and NLRA regulations require documented consent for severance agreements, arbitration clauses, non-competes, and benefits elections. The legal standard — "knowing and voluntary consent" — requires more than a checkbox.

**Specific workflows:**
- Offer letters and onboarding packets
- Benefits elections
- Severance agreements
- Non-disclosure and non-compete agreements
- Remote I-9 verification

**Best approach:** Target PEOs (Professional Employer Organizations) and HR software vendors via Flex API. One partnership reaches mid-market employers at scale.

---

### Sector 6: Government / Public Sector (Long game)

**Why it matters:**  
Federal agencies and state governments are under mandate to modernize citizen-facing digital services. NIST SP 800-63 requires higher assurance levels for high-risk transactions. Contract values are large and sticky.

**Best approach:** 12–18 month sales cycle. Keep on radar; do not allocate paid media budget now.

## What the Team Needs to Answer (Internal Research)

These six questions sharpen the entire strategy. Even rough answers from a 30-minute conversation with sales and a CSM would transform a good plan into a precise one.

| # | Question | Why it matters |
|---|----------|---------------|
| 1 | **Who are SIGNiX's best current customers?** Firm type, size, and which workflow they use SIGNiX for | Two or three examples create a pattern to replicate across sectors |
| 2 | **Does SIGNiX have compliance certifications or published case studies?** | Trust signals for landing pages and outbound emails — especially for regulated sectors |
| 3 | **How exactly does Fraud Alert work?** Specifically: which FINRA guidance was it built against, and what does the output look like? | Makes the compliance claim precise and defensible in ad copy and sales conversations |
| 4 | **What do CSMs actually do day-to-day?** What problems do they most commonly solve? | Raw material for the partnership-first message — best copy comes from real conversations |
| 5 | **Why have deals been lost recently?** Top three reasons prospects chose a competitor | Tells you which objections to address in messaging; "they went with DocuSign" is not enough |
| 6 | **What is a typical deal ACV for a non-notary customer?** | Determines how much we can spend per lead before the math breaks |
