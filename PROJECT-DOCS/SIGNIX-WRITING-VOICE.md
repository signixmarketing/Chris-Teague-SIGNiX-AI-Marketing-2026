# SIGNiX Writing Voice

Plain language and human tone for all SIGNiX content.

**Last updated:** 2026-04-02

---

## The goal

Write at a 6th–7th grade reading level. Sound like a person talking in a meeting, not a report being filed. Most readers skim. Make every sentence earn its place.

---

## Always do this

- **Short sentences.** 15 words or fewer on average.
- **Active voice.** "We generate leads" not "Leads are generated."
- **Lead with the reader.** Start with "you" or their problem. Put the company second.
- **Front-load.** Put the key point at the start of the sentence. Don't bury it.
- **One idea per sentence.** If a sentence has two ideas, make it two sentences.

---

## Never do this

| Instead of | Use |
|---|---|
| Em dash (—) | A period or comma |
| Leverage | Use |
| Utilize | Use |
| Ensure | Make sure |
| Comprehensive | Full or complete |
| Tailored | Built for |
| Furthermore / Moreover | And (or start a new sentence) |
| Disproportionate | Too much |
| "It is important to note that…" | Just say the thing |
| Passive voice | Active voice |

---

## The em dash problem

Em dashes are the most common AI tell. Most people don't use them in normal speech or writing. When readers see them, they start to wonder if the content is machine-made.

**Bad:** "This plan targets FI buyers — the segment with the highest ACV."
**Good:** "This plan targets FI buyers. They have the highest ACV."

---

## Copyhackers: three techniques to use

These come from Joanna Wiebe at [Copyhackers](https://copyhackers.com):

1. **Front-load.** The most important words go at the start of each line. Readers scan left. Don't waste that space.
2. **You-first.** Rewrite every sentence with "you" at the start. Then edit the ones that sound robotic. This forces you to keep the reader first.
3. **Even if.** After a benefit, add "even if [the objection]." Example: "You can replace DocuSign even if your team is already trained on it." Addresses doubt before it forms.

---

## Plain Language rules (from PlainLanguage.gov)

- Write for your audience. What do they already know? What do they need?
- Use common words. One or two syllables when possible.
- Cut filler. "In order to" becomes "to." "At this point in time" becomes "now."
- Short paragraphs. Three to five sentences is plenty.
- Use "we" and "you." It sounds more human than "the organization" or "the client."

---

## The gut-check test

Before you finish any marketing or business content, ask:

> "Would I say this out loud in a meeting?"

If the answer is no, rewrite it.

---

## Words that signal AI (avoid these)

- Delve, underscore, leverage, utilize, facilitate
- "It is worth noting that…"
- "In conclusion…"
- "This comprehensive solution…"
- "Tailored to your unique needs"
- Any sentence with three parallel clauses separated by em dashes
