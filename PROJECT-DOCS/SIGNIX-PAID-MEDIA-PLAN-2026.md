## 1. The Case for Reallocation

### Current state

SIGNiX's paid media spend (~$5,000/month) is concentrated on Google Ads targeting individual notary and RON queries. These campaigns attract individual notary professionals — low ACV, high-touch customers who require a full sales close plus dedicated CSM onboarding.

**The economics are broken at the unit level:**

When the salary cost of a sales representative and Client Success Manager (Gina) is divided by the percentage of time spent per notary lead — from first touch through closed deal and full onboarding — the contribution margin per notary customer is likely near zero or negative.

**Key signals:**
- Individual notary deals: low ACV, one-time onboarding cost, low recurring transaction volume
- Staff time per deal: disproportionately high relative to deal value
- Scalability: none — each deal requires the same manual motion regardless of volume

### Proposed state

Redirect spend toward three sectors where SIGNiX wins — regulated industries where a click is legally insufficient and identity-bound signatures are required:

- **Financial services / Healthcare:** Banks, credit unions, broker-dealers, RIAs, and health systems under FINRA/SEC/HIPAA requirements. A failed audit or disputed transaction costs more than years of SIGNiX.
- **Wealth management:** RIAs, broker-dealers, and financial advisors executing beneficiary changes, IRA distributions, and account events where a disputed signature is a legal and financial event.
- **Legal / Debt collection:** Law firms and collection agencies under FDCPA and state consent requirements. Proving who signed — and when — is the difference between a valid agreement and a disputed one.

> **Note:** Real estate / mortgage was removed as a target sector. New mortgage customer acquisition faces too many barriers (regulatory complexity, long sales cycles, and existing dominant vendor relationships) to be a productive paid media target at this budget level.

**The shift does not require a budget increase.** The CEO has approved $3,000/month on Google only. LinkedIn is Phase 2 — launched once Google performance is proven at 60 days.

## 3. Google Search Ads Strategy ($3,000/month)

### Strategic principle

Avoid head-to-head competition on generic e-sign terms (e.g., "electronic signature") where DocuSign and Adobe dominate with multi-million-dollar budgets. Compete on specificity: regulated industry, compliance urgency, authentication, and niche competitor displacement.

**The three sectors we target:** financial services (FINRA/SEC), healthcare (HIPAA), and real estate/mortgage. Each has buyers who search for compliance-specific terms with high purchase intent and low competition.

### Ad message principles

**Lead with fear. Close with partnership.**

The buyer's fear opens the door. The human relationship closes the deal.

- **Fear-first openers:** "One disputed signature you can't prove costs more than years of SIGNiX." / "FINRA is flagging electronic records right now. Can you prove who signed?" / "AI fraud is targeting financial accounts. A click is not a signature."
- **Partnership close:** "SIGNiX assigns you a CSM who knows your workflows before a problem happens."
- Each bucket lands on a **dedicated landing page** matched to the search intent — not the generic homepage
- Authentication and compliance buckets: tie to Mike's email campaign and Fraud Alert
- Never lead with PKI, features, or product specs — lead with the consequence of not having it

## 3a. Keywords Tested and Removed

The following keywords were submitted to Google Keyword Planner for 12-month volume analysis (March 2025 – February 2026). All returned zero or near-zero search volume and have been removed from active campaigns. They are documented here for reference.

| Keyword | Former Bucket | Avg Monthly Searches | YoY Change | Reason Removed |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `e signature for banks` | Industry / Use-Case | 20 | **–75%** | Dying term. Volume declined sharply year over year. |
| `digital signature mortgage docs` | Industry / Use-Case | 0 | N/A | Zero volume. Mortgage sector also removed as a target. |
| `remote online notary platform financial institutions` | RON Institutional | 0 | N/A | Zero volume. Too niche as a search phrase. |
| `e signature rollover forms` | Wealth Management | 0 | N/A | Zero volume. Buyers search the document type, not the signing action. |
| `ira distribution e sign` | Wealth Management | 0 | N/A | Zero volume. Industry shorthand — not how buyers search. |
| `kba electronic signature` | Authentication / Fraud | 0 | N/A | Zero volume. "KBA" is internal industry jargon. Buyers don't know the acronym. |
| `e signature identity verification` | Authentication / Fraud | 0 | N/A | Zero volume. Wrong word order for how buyers search. Replaced with `identity verification electronic signature`. |
| `ai fraud e signature` | Authentication / Fraud | 0 | N/A | Zero volume. Search behavior for this topic has not yet developed. Re-test in Q3 2026. |

**What the data confirmed:** The two keywords with validated volume were `beneficiary change form` (390/month, +50% YoY) and `hipaa compliant e-signature` (210/month, +191% in 3 months). Both are retained and treated as anchor terms for their respective buckets.

## 6. Leading and Lagging Indicators

### Leading indicators — check weekly

| Indicator | What it tells you | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Impression share by keyword bucket | Are we visible on priority terms? | >40% on priority buckets |
| CTR by bucket | Are the right people clicking? | 3–6% for B2B search |
| Average CPC trends | Is spend efficient or inflating? | Monitor week-over-week |
| Landing page bounce rate | Is traffic finding what they expected? | <60% |
| Form fill / demo request rate | Are visitors converting? | 2–5% of paid clicks |
| LinkedIn engagement rate | Are the right people stopping? | >0.5% sponsored content |
| Lead qualification rate | Are leads matching ICP? | >40% qualified |
| Time from lead to first sales touchpoint | Is the handoff working? | <48 hours |

### Lagging indicators — check monthly and quarterly

| Indicator | What it tells you | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per qualified lead (CPL) by channel | Which channel delivers the most efficient leads? | Split by Google bucket + LinkedIn audience |
| MQLs per month | Is pipeline building? | Define MQL criteria before launch |
| Sales-accepted leads (SALs) | Is the sales team seeing quality? | Track rep feedback per lead source |
| Pipeline sourced ($) from paid channels | What's the revenue potential? | Attribute in HubSpot |
| Deals closed from paid sources | What's the actual return? | 90–180 day lag expected |
| Average deal ACV by lead source | Are paid leads higher value? | Compare vs. notary lead ACV |
| Staff hours per closed deal by source | Hidden cost metric; proves notary lead problem | Estimate with rep + CSM time logs |
| Transaction volume from new paid-sourced customers | Does it generate recurring revenue? | The ultimate SIGNiX metric |
