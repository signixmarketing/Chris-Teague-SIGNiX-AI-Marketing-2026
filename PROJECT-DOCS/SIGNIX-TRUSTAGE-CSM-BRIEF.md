## The Original Email (Summary)

TruStage sent a pre-call request with three asks:

1. **Client success stories and support ticket data** to add to their sales talk track — volume, topics, comparison to other clients/industries. Their assumption: integrated solutions (TruStage/CSI clients) have lower ticket volume than standalone MyDoX clients.

2. **Proactive client feedback calls** — Mike S was supposed to call into the shared client base to surface feedback, good and bad. TruStage wants to know if it happened. If not, can he do it now? Or should they run it jointly?

3. **HR client complaint** — Anecdotal feedback that HR clients are frustrated with "complex signature sections and limitations on field size." TruStage wants SIGNiX's visibility and thoughts.

---

## Why This Matters Strategically

This is a growth opportunity, not just a support task. TruStage is asking for ammunition to sell SIGNiX harder to their client base. A strong response can:

- Strengthen the TruStage partnership directly
- Surface dormant FIs who have SIGNiX but haven't activated authentication (the **depth lever**)
- Identify HR as a workflow where SIGNiX is being used but may be leaking churn — and a potential expansion segment (the **width lever**)

The proactive calling ask also aligns with SIGNiX's partnership-first positioning. These calls are not sales calls — they are relationship conversations that happen to reveal activation opportunities.

---

## Game Plan: Three Actions

### Action 1 — Support ticket data pull
**Owner:** CSM (Gina)  
**Timeline:** Ready for next weekly call (within 5 business days)

Pull from your ticketing system:
- Total ticket volume over the last 6–12 months, segmented by: integrated TruStage/CSI clients vs. standalone MyDoX clients
- Top 5 ticket categories by frequency (e.g., field placement, authentication setup, signer issues, doc formatting, field size)
- Average resolution time and any repeat ticket patterns

Package it as:
- Two or three sentences on what the data shows overall
- One or two "most common issue + how we resolved it" proof points
- A clean comparison of integrated vs. standalone ticket volume (confirm or correct TruStage's assumption with real numbers)

---

### Action 2 — Mike S proactive calling plan
**Owner:** CSM + Mike S (loop in CEO for decision)  
**Timeline:** Confirm direction this week; calls begin within 2–3 weeks

First, establish the current state: has Mike started this outreach or not?

**If Mike has not started:**
Propose a joint SIGNiX + TruStage approach. Structure:
- 10–15 minute calls with 10–20 shared FI contacts
- Goal is explicit: surface both good experiences and friction points — not a sales call
- Call guide should include 4–5 open questions: What workflows are you signing today? What's working well? Where does the process slow down? Have you explored authentication features?
- These conversations naturally surface authentication activation opportunities without a hard pitch

**If Mike has started:**
Share what was found and propose formalizing the process as a regular quarterly touch with TruStage.

Either way, the recommendation to TruStage is a **joint approach**. It strengthens the partnership and gives SIGNiX direct access to FI contacts it is already cleared to reach.

---

### Action 3 — HR field size and signature complexity complaint
**Owner:** CSM + Product team  
**Timeline:** Initial response within 5 days; product answer within 2 weeks

**Immediate step:** Check the ticketing system specifically for HR-related tickets mentioning field size, signature section layout, or document complexity. Even two or three tickets with that theme confirm the anecdotal feedback and gives TruStage a credible answer.

**Product escalation:** Flag this to the product team and get a clear answer to:
- Is the field size limitation a known constraint?
- Is there a current workaround?
- Is it on the product roadmap to fix?

Do not let this sit as an unanswered product question. If HR clients are hitting a wall on field sizes, that is churn risk in a segment SIGNiX wants to grow into. It needs a product owner and a timeline.
